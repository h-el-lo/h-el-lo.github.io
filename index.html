03 — Background

About

I'm an embedded systems engineer who builds things from the ground up. My projects start at the schematic and end when the firmware is stable enough to take on stage.

Most of my work sits at the intersection of electronics and music — MIDI controllers, rebuilt instruments, and firmware tools that let musicians focus on performing instead of troubleshooting hardware. I've had hardware I designed deployed in live gigs, which is a different kind of stress-test.

I work across the full stack: PCB layout, hardware assembly, firmware (C++), and desktop tooling (Python). I iterate in the field — soldering iron in one hand, serial monitor in the other.

Currently based in Akure, Ondo State. Open to embedded systems and IoT roles where speed, ownership, and technical excellence are valued.
